description_en: |
  Penedo House is an exercise in dialogue between architecture and nature. Set on a granite hillside in Braga, the dwelling is organised around the existing rock, which cuts through the interior as a sculptural element.

  The chosen materials — exposed concrete, local stone, and chestnut wood — ensure visual continuity between the built and the natural. Light enters in a controlled manner through south-facing openings, creating serene spaces that shift throughout the day.
